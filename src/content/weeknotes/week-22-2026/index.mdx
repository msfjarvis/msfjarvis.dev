## Personal Projects

Big week for the tens of users of my [lobsters](https://lobste.rs) app [Claw](https://msfjarvis.dev/g/compose-lobsters) as I had to make two releases in quick succession. The first release had been baking for nearly 2 months, and added the ability to log into the app to lay the groundwork for building interactive features in the future. I also switched the app over to not rely on the JSON API the site exposes due to a Ruby on Rails misfeature (according to the site admin) and instead just scrape the HTML itself. As my luck would have it, the markup changed literally 2 days after I made the release after having been perfectly stable for _weeks_ since I made the initial swap, so I had to do an emergency hotfix. Grrr

I've continued hacking on the app, making a big range of changes to bring it up to parity with the website experience. In no particular order:

- Screenshot testing to make it easier to do iterative improvements
- Changed the comments UI and UX to closely resemble the defunct Reddit client _Boost_ — big thanks to Yash for that suggestion
- (Unsuccessfully) [attempt](https://github.com/msfjarvis/compose-lobsters/pull/1183) to move the parsing logic to a [Zipline](https://github.com/cashapp/zipline)-backed module that would let me hot patch it without making users update the app
- (In progress) add the ability for users to [reply to comments](https://github.com/msfjarvis/compose-lobsters/pull/1184). I paused work on it to write this weeknote!

## Clanker things

I talked [last week](/weeknotes/week-21-2026) about experimenting with `agentmemory` to remove the need for `AGENTS.md` files, so far that has yielded exactly 0 value. I haven't had any luck in steering LLMs into autonomously persisting facts and decisions into memory, and `AGENTS.md` files themselves have been feeling useless as LLMs seem to mostly ignore them even in projects that do have the files. I did some tweaks to the prompt today to improve `agentmemory` usage, and will report back next week on how that went.

I also made one major-but-not-really change to the harness by [vendoring every single skill I use](https://git.msfjarvis.dev/msfjarvis/my-pi/commit/3045be0afc8bff4cea4fb148e39840bd360b9861) into the Git repository itself rather than having them be symlinks to an untracked `~/.agents` directory. This makes the setup more portable and easier to make modifications to should I need to.

I don't always agree with [Armin Ronacher](https://lucumr.pocoo.org), but I like to keep up with his excellent writing regardless. This week he posted a [great article](https://lucumr.pocoo.org/2026/5/26/clankers/) about his persistent use of the word "Clankers" for what the AI pilled call _agents_, and I found myself nodding along to everything he was saying. I'm not going to do him the injustice of distilling his argument down to a couple of lines, so please go give it a read.
